Recycle Cardboard

To properly recycle cardboard, you should first empty it of any packing materials or trash, keeping any tape or shipping labels on the box, then flatten it to save space.

Tutorial to recycle cardboard

Step1: Empty and Flatten.
Remove any contents like packing peanuts or bubble wrap, but leave tape and labels on the box. Flatten the box to take up less space in your recycling bin.

Step2: Keep Clean and Dry
Ensure the cardboard is free of food debris, grease, or moisture, which can contaminate the recycling process.

Step3: Bundle or Bag (if necessary)
If your recycling bin is full, you can bundle or bag flattened cardboard to keep it organized and prevent it from being tossed in the regular trash.

Step4: Check Local Guidelines
Recycling practices can vary by location. It's always best to check with your local waste management or recycling authority to ensure you're following their specific instructions.

Step5: Consider Repurposing
If you're not sure about recycling, consider repurposing cardboard for projects like art, crafts, or organizing.

Recycling paper

Turning paper into new paper products.

Tutorial to recycle Paper

Step 1: Collect the old papers
The whole process of recycling paper starts with collecting them.
Step 2: Sorting them.
After we have collected our papers, we need to sort them. Certain paper products can be processed differntly depending on type.
Step 3: Shredding and pulping
After we've done sorting our different type of papers, it is shredded down into small scarps. Large amount of water and chemicals, like peroxide, sodium hydroxide, and sodium slilcate can be added to the shreds to futher break down the paper into separate paper fibers. The result is a mushy concoction known as pulp, the raw material that is used to make recycled paper.
Step 4: Inking
Once we are done pulping, the pulp can be transfered into a larde container tank and the chemicals we added and the air bubbles help remove the dye and inks from the pulp. In this step, we can use different color dyes to create prefered color paper of your choice.
Step 5: Drying
The final step in recycling process is drying. Once then pulp has de-inked and dyed, we can use strainers or rollers to squeese out excess water from the mixture. After that, we can dry them out in the sun and from there, we can use the paper to however you wish to use it again.
