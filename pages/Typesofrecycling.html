Material Recycling

Tips

Step 1: Collect recyclable materials such as paper, plastic, metal, and glass.

Step 2: Sort the materials by type to ensure proper recycling processing.

Step 3: Send the materials to a recycling facility where they are shredded, melted, or crushed.

Step 4: Reprocess the raw materials into new products like paper, containers, or packaging.

Note: This is the most common form of recycling and helps conserve natural resources.

Chemical Recycling

Tips

Step 1: Collect plastic waste that cannot be mechanically recycled.

Step 2: Use processes like pyrolysis or depolymerization to break down plastic polymers.

Step 3: Extract the basic chemicals or monomers.

Step 4: Use these recovered materials to produce new, high-quality plastics or products.

Note: This method helps recycle mixed or dirty plastics that would otherwise go to landfills.

Thermal Recycling

Tips

Step 1: Gather non-recyclable waste materials, especially plastics and packaging.

Step 2: Burn the waste in a controlled incinerator at high temperatures.

Step 3: Capture the heat energy produced during combustion.

Step 4: Convert the heat into electricity or industrial steam.

Note: Emission control systems are essential to reduce air pollution.

Organic Recycling

Tips

Step 1: Collect kitchen waste like vegetable peels, eggshells, and coffee grounds.

Step 2: Add garden waste like leaves, grass, and small branches.

Step 3: Combine the organic waste in a compost bin or outdoor pile.

Step 4: Stir the pile regularly to allow air circulation.
